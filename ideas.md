# TryBello Advertorial — Design Brainstorm

## Context
A direct-response advertorial for TryBello Hair Helper Spray. The copy is a first-person confessional by a celebrity hairstylist named Renée Halsted. The goal is to make it look like a legitimate editorial/news article published on a wellness blog or investigative journalism site — not a sales page.

## Idea 1: "The Investigative Wellness Journal"

**Design Movement:** Long-form investigative journalism meets premium wellness editorial (think The Cut meets The Atlantic)

**Core Principles:**
- Maximum editorial legitimacy — every design choice should make this feel like a real published article
- High-contrast typographic hierarchy: the copy IS the design
- Warm, humanistic color palette that evokes trust and intimacy
- Strategic use of "evidence" visual language (document-style callouts, study citations, pull quotes)

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: warm off-white `#F8F5F0` — feels like quality newsprint
- Body text: near-black `#1A1A1A` — serious, readable
- Accent: deep forest green `#1B4332` — authority, health, nature
- Highlight: warm amber `#C8860A` — draws eye to key claims, feels like a highlighter
- Danger/warning: muted brick red `#8B2E2E` — used for failed treatments only

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Narrow editorial column (max 680px) centered on page
- Fake publication header bar at top: logo, category tag ("HEALTH & WELLNESS"), date, share icons
- Section headers are large, left-aligned, bold serif — break up the long scroll
- Pull quotes styled as large italic blockquotes with a left amber border
- Ingredient cards and comparison tables styled like infographic inserts in a magazine

**Signature Elements:**
- A "Published by" byline with author avatars (Renée + Dr. Holmes) at the very top
- "BREAKING" or "EXCLUSIVE" badge on the header
- Sticky "reading progress" bar at the top
- Inline CTA buttons styled to look like "sponsored content" disclosure boxes

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Smooth scroll, no jarring transitions
- Sticky bottom bar with countdown timer that appears after 30% scroll
- CTA buttons have subtle pulse animation to draw the eye

**Animation:**
- Fade-in on scroll for section images and callout boxes
- No flashy animations — everything should feel editorial and restrained

**Typography System:**
- Headlines: Playfair Display (serif) — authoritative, journalistic
- Body: Lora (serif) — warm, readable, editorial
- Captions/labels: DM Sans (sans-serif) — clinical contrast

## Idea 2: "The Confessional Longread"

**Design Movement:** Personal essay platform meets investigative health blog (think Medium's premium stories or Substack)

**Core Principles:**
- The narrator's voice IS the brand — design must feel deeply personal and intimate
- Minimal chrome, maximum content — the story should feel like it's being told directly to the reader
- Typographic warmth: large, generous line-height, comfortable reading experience
- Visual evidence breaks up the text at emotional peaks

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: pure cream `#FFFDF7`
- Text: warm charcoal `#2C2C2C`
- Accent: dusty rose / mauve `#C2847A` — feminine, emotional, warm
- Secondary accent: sage green `#5C7A6A` — health, nature, credibility
- CTA: deep hunter green `#1A3A2A`

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Full-bleed hero image at top with headline overlaid
- Single-column reading layout, generous margins
- Section images are full-width or left-floated with text wrapping
- "Chapter" style section breaks with large decorative numbers or ornamental dividers

**Signature Elements:**
- Author bio card at the top (photo, credentials, "18 years in Hollywood")
- Emotional pull quotes in large italic serif, centered
- "Confession" styling on the opening — slightly different background tint, as if it's a letter

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Estimated reading time shown at the top
- Smooth entrance animations on images as you scroll
- CTA sections feel like natural pauses in the narrative, not interruptions

**Animation:**
- Images fade up on scroll
- CTA button has a gentle shimmer effect

**Typography System:**
- Headlines: Cormorant Garamond (elegant serif) — confessional, literary
- Body: Source Serif 4 — comfortable long-form reading
- UI elements: Outfit (geometric sans) — modern contrast

## Idea 3: "The Wellness News Report" ← SELECTED

**Design Movement:** Digital health news publication meets premium editorial (think Healthline's editorial features or WebMD's investigative pieces)

**Core Principles:**
- Unimpeachable editorial credibility — looks like a real news website with a masthead, byline, and category tags
- Clean, structured layout with clear visual hierarchy that guides the reader through the long-form content
- "Evidence-based" visual language: study callout boxes, ingredient cards, comparison charts
- Emotional resonance through photography: each section image should mirror the emotional tone of the copy

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: `#FAFAF8` — clean, slightly warm white (not clinical)
- Primary text: `#1C1C1E` — near-black for maximum readability
- Publication accent: deep forest green `#1B4D3E` — authority, wellness, trust
- Highlight/amber: `#B8860B` — used for key claims, callout borders, pull quotes
- Warning/red: `#C0392B` — failed treatments, competitor failures only
- Light card background: `#F2F0EB` — ingredient cards, testimonial boxes

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Fake publication header: "WellnessReport" masthead, nav links, search icon
- Article metadata bar: category pill ("HAIR HEALTH"), author avatars, date, read time
- Narrow editorial column (max 700px) with generous side margins
- Section images are full-width within the column, with captions
- Ingredient/study callouts styled as "Editor's Note" or "Research Highlight" boxes
- Comparison tables styled as editorial data tables

**Signature Elements:**
- Sticky reading progress bar at the top
- "SPONSORED CONTENT" disclosure badge (builds trust through transparency)
- Pull quotes with large amber left-border and italic serif text
- Author credential cards (circular photo, name, title) at top and bottom

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Scroll-triggered fade-in for images and callout boxes
- Sticky bottom CTA bar appears after 40% scroll depth
- CTA buttons styled as "editorial" — not garish, but clearly actionable

**Animation:**
- Subtle fade-up on scroll for images and data cards
- Countdown timer has a ticking animation
- CTA button has a very subtle pulse

**Typography System:**
- Headlines: Playfair Display Bold — journalistic authority
- Body: Merriweather — warm, highly readable serif for long-form
- UI/Labels: Inter — clean, modern sans for metadata and badges

## Selected Approach: Idea 3 — "The Wellness News Report"

This approach best serves the advertorial's goals: it maximizes editorial credibility (making the reader feel they're reading a real investigative health article), uses a warm but authoritative color palette that matches the brand's existing green/amber identity, and provides clear structural landmarks (section images, callout boxes, comparison tables) that break up the long copy and keep the reader engaged.
